Cyborg or Rooms?

January 2026

I have always been part cyborg. I had my first Fitbit in seventh grade, my first iPhone in sixth, and my first Apple Watch in ninth grade.

Thinking about wearables through the definition of a cyborg is interesting. It raises questions about the nature of humanity and our relationship with technology, and it highlights how often humans wear technology to enhance life without fully considering how connected that makes us to the internet.

I still wear a fitness device every day, but I have opted for a Whoop, which noticeably does not have a screen. After being part cyborg for so long, I am now looking for ways to distance myself from screens.

The concept of "rooms" in the article is also fascinating. The idea that physical spaces are intertwined with digital experiences is something I have been thinking about a lot lately. As I try to build a more balanced relationship with technology, I also consider how my environment shapes that relationship.

It is interesting that I have felt part cyborg for so long, a term that can feel alien, yet I had not really considered the room experience before, even though it feels more human.
