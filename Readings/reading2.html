The New Turing Test

January 2026

Asking students to strive for three things to bring humanity back into art seems like a noble goal: to show that art came from someone, to make art for someone, and to show that art belongs in a particular context.

As a student hearing this from a professor, I would feel inspired to create something that is innately human. As discussed in the article, the feeling that art has been composed rather than created seems as though art has been predicted.

AI models are trained on large existing sets of data. Some could argue that makes AI human because it is a cumulative summary of many human responses. AI is trained to predict, but it is not trained to understand the feeling of loneliness, loss, or immense peacefulness, at least not yet.

This raises the question of whether art created by AI can ever truly be considered human, or if it will always lack the depth of genuine human experience. I also enjoyed the comparison between creating and training for a race or triathlon: both require dedication, practice, and a deep understanding of oneself.
