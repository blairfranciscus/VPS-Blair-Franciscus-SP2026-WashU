The New Turing Test (Revision)

January 2026

Asking students to strive for three things to bring humanity back into art still feels like a strong framework: show the art came from someone, make it for someone, and place it in a meaningful context.

As a student, that perspective encourages me to create work that is not only technically interesting but also emotionally grounded. The article's distinction between composed and created work also stayed with me, because AI systems often feel predictive even when the output is visually compelling.

AI is trained on human-made datasets, which makes the boundary between human and machine creativity complex. But prediction alone does not equal lived understanding. The emotional depth behind loneliness, loss, and peace is still deeply tied to human experience.

I also appreciated the comparison between creative process and athletic training. Both demand discipline, repetition, reflection, and a willingness to move through discomfort in order to produce something meaningful.
