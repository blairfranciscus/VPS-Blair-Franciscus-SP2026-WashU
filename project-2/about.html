The Language of Flowers

The Garden The Catalog About

Project Motivation

My motivation for this project was to explore a creative system of communication built through symbolism. The Victorian "language of flowers," known as floriography, allowed people to express complex emotions and social messages through bouquets and individual flowers. Instead of speaking directly, individuals could communicate feelings such as love, grief, admiration, jealousy, or loyalty through carefully selected plants.

This project showcases how symbolic communication systems can be translated into modern digital interfaces. By combining historical references with interactive design, the goal is to explore how visual symbols and structured information can help convey meaning in intuitive and expressive ways.

Book Inspiration

Two source texts shaped both the emotional concept and the visual system of this website.

Narrative Source

The Language of Flowers

Author: Vanessa Diffenbaugh

The Language of Flowers is a contemporary novel that reintroduces readers to the Victorian tradition of floriography through the story of a young woman who uses flowers to express emotions she struggles to communicate directly.

Throughout the book, different plants symbolize specific feelings, and bouquets become a subtle language for expressing love, grief, forgiveness, and hope.

The novel demonstrates how symbolic systems can carry emotional depth and personal meaning. It inspired the idea that flowers can act as a form of emotional vocabulary, where individuals construct messages through combinations of symbols.

Reference Source

Floriography

Author: Jessica Roux

Floriography: An Illustrated Guide to the Victorian Language of Flowers is an illustrated botanical reference that catalogues dozens of flowers and their symbolic meanings from Victorian-era floral dictionaries.

Each flower entry includes its traditional meaning, historical context, and botanical illustration. The book presents floriography as both a visual and linguistic system.

Its structured format - combining imagery, taxonomy, and symbolic meaning - influenced the visual language of this website. The botanical plate style, centered compositions, and minimal layouts used in this project draw heavily from the design approach of Roux's work.
