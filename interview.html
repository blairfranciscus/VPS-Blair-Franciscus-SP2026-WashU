Interview Assignment

Digital Platforms Interview

A conversation across marketing, healthcare, aviation, and law

Introduction

This interview compares perspectives across marketing, healthcare, aviation, and law. Participants discussed the platforms they use, what works well, what creates friction, and how AI may reshape professional communication while trust and human relationships remain central.

Sydney Tatar

Student, University of Richmond (Marketing & Business)

Erin Tatar

SVP, Consultant Relations, Laturn (Healthcare)

Edmund Tatar

Senior Managing Director, GMT Global Republic Aviation

Rebecca Franciscus

Former SEC Attorney and Legal Trustee

1. What digital platforms do you use to communicate or share information in your work?

How each participant uses digital tools to organize communication and build trust.

Sydney Tatar

As a student, I mainly use Blackboard (now transitioning to Canvas), Workday, BannerWeb, and Google Suite. In a consulting course, I also use EdgeSource to coordinate with local business clients and track milestones. These systems work best when they are simple and easy to learn quickly.

Erin Tatar

We rely on Microsoft tools for internal and external communication, plus Highspot for enablement and Gong for call recording and AI-generated insights. Gong improved follow-up quality, but adoption required change management and clear privacy expectations.

Edmund Tatar

Our firm is small, so we mostly use traditional Microsoft Office and Teams workflows. We tested platforms like Salesforce, but for our size the setup cost and complexity often outweigh the benefits.

Rebecca Franciscus

At the SEC, I worked with complaint intake systems that interfaced with the public and other agencies, with backend categorization for issues like crypto fraud and Ponzi schemes. In private trust work today, I use Microsoft tools, video platforms, and Addepar for portfolio reporting.

2. Platform Effectiveness + Friction

Best-use examples and key usability breakdowns.

2A. What is the best digital platform you've used and what made it effective?

Sydney Tatar

Google Suite has been the strongest platform in school because it is collaborative, shareable, and familiar to most users. For student teams, that ease of collaboration is the biggest strength.

Erin Tatar

Gong has been most impactful. It automatically captures calls, produces strong summaries, and integrates with Salesforce records. Once barriers to adoption were removed, the value became obvious to teams.

Edmund Tatar

For our company, familiar Microsoft tools are most effective because they are stable, universal, and require little onboarding. Simplicity has more value than adding large platforms that do not match our scale.

Rebecca Franciscus

Appian at the SEC worked well when iterated over time. Each update improved data collection and case categorization, showing that platform success often comes from continuous refinement rather than one launch.

2B. What platforms have been the most difficult to use?

Sydney Tatar

Blackboard has been the most difficult due to login issues, inconsistent announcements, and a less intuitive interface. Those usability issues directly impact student communication and workflow.

Erin Tatar

Salesforce is the biggest challenge. It is hard to structure correctly from the start, difficult to rebuild later, and often creates data hygiene issues because updating records can feel manual and cumbersome.

Edmund Tatar

Some tools now force AI features into routine tasks. For example, document tools can become less usable when AI interrupts straightforward workflows that users already know well.

Rebecca Franciscus

The same Appian ecosystem became difficult when whistleblower workflows were over-structured. Required checkboxes reduced flexibility, so teams had to redesign the system to support case-by-case differences.

3. How do you think digital communication and AI will shape the future of your profession?

Future outlook on AI, privacy, trust, and professional communication.

Sydney Tatar

In education, AI guardrails are a major concern. Schools need to support efficiency while still ensuring students learn core skills independently. Accessibility across different backgrounds and learning needs also remains essential.

Erin Tatar

AI is strongest as a synthesis tool that pulls information from scattered systems into usable insights. Adoption will depend on change management, but trust-based relationships will still drive major business decisions.

Edmund Tatar

In aviation investment, private market data limits what public AI tools can do accurately. New professionals should learn AI, but they must also be strong in traditional skills like spreadsheets, documents, and presentations.

Rebecca Franciscus

In both government and trust work, confidentiality limits AI usage. Internal systems are expensive to build, and clients expect privacy. AI can support ideation, but trust remains person-to-person.
