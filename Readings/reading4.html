Websites as a Shifting House

February 4, 2026

Intentionality in the online world is a dying value. With easily accessible ways to share opinions, like Twitter and TikTok, the focus has shifted from meaningful discourse to quick, attention-grabbing content.

When I think about what I want to put out into the world, I think about the intentionality behind an entire website that represents me. It requires more than a quick thought typed and posted to a social feed.

There are many choices one has to make that can digitally convey identity, passion, mission, and values. Thinking about my personal website, whether it is a shelf, rock, garden, or puddle, I question if I am truly representing myself in the way I want to be seen.

Maybe I want to be seen as a rock, solid and unchanging. Or perhaps as a garden, constantly growing and evolving. Each choice reflects a different aspect of identity and how I wish to engage with the world.

Or maybe this is more about process: sometimes I have enough to fill a lake with my online presence, while other times I feel like a puddle, barely making a splash.
